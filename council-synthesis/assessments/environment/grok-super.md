**Assessment of Environment Domain Coverage**

**1. BLIND SPOTS**

The 12 entries collectively miss:
- Urban metabolism and circular economy at city scale (no coverage of industrial symbiosis, construction material loops, or building lifecycle decarbonization).
- Soil microbiome and regenerative agriculture beyond precision fermentation (no mention of cover-cropping networks, mycorrhizal restoration, or the 2020s soil carbon marketplace explosion).
- Ocean CDR and alkalinity enhancement (missing entirely; only turtle recovery and vague marine reserves).
- Freshwater scarcity and aquifer recovery (Klamath and Yangtze are river-specific; no mention of the Ogallala, Indo-Gangetic, or North China Plain depletion curves).
- Phosphorus and nitrogen cycle overshoot (planetary boundary literature is referenced only obliquely).
- Invasive species acceleration under climate change and synthetic biology containment risks.
- Deep decarbonization of hard-to-abate sectors (cement, steel, aviation) and their land-use implications.
- Governance of solar radiation management research.

- **Dr. Walter Jehne** (Australia): Soil microbiologist; founder of Healthy Soils Australia; demonstrated 0.5–1 tC/ha/yr sequestration via fungal networks and hydrology repair at scale.
- **Dr. Karen Holl** (UC Santa Cruz): Led rigorous tropical forest restoration trials showing active planting often underperforms natural regeneration when seed sources exist; shaped IUCN guidelines.

**3. SHADOW GAPS**

Unaddressed shadows:
- **Green gentrification** and exclusionary conservation (wealthy coastal cities using “restoration” to justify displacement).
- **Bioenergy with CCS (BECCS)** land-use trap: models still assume 500–1000 Mha of energy crops, directly contradicting the “80% land liberation” thesis in entry 01.
- **AI/ML rebound effects**: training the biodiversity-sensing stack (entry 11) at planetary scale could consume gigatons of embodied energy and rare earths.
- **Ocean governance vacuum**: 60% of ocean is high seas with no enforcement mechanism; satellite monitoring (entry 10) cannot substitute for property rights or patrols.
- **Temporal mismatch**: restoration timelines (decades for soil, centuries for old-growth function) versus political cycles and venture capital horizons.
- **Equity in data ownership**: the digitization entries (07, 08, 11) acknowledge extraction but miss how frontier AI models themselves are now trained on proprietary biodiversity datasets without consent.

The immune system is weakest on **land-use competition** between rewilding, BECCS, urban expansion, and smallholder justice.

The null +0.03 actionability delta exists because entries celebrate outcomes or critique mechanisms but never supply replicable intervention design, sequencing, or failure-tolerant execution protocols.

**6. CONFIDENCE CALIBRATION**

Scores are systematically overconfident on recovery speed and underconfident on irreversibility. The 0.55 confidence on RethinkX’s 80% land claim (entry 01) is reasonable, but 0.92–0.95 on policy successes (entry 02) ignores selection bias—we only have solved-crisis case studies. MethaneSAT’s failure mode (lost contact June 2025) is mentioned yet the entry still scores 0.83–0.90 on the sensing stack. Lowest confidence (0.78 on bio-acoustic shadow) appropriately reflects contested evidence, but highest scores (0.98 on Montreal ratification, 0.95 on bald eagles) treat completed victories as more predictive than they are for novel problems like novel ecosystems.
