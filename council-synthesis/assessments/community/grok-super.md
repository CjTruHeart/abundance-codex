**Assessment of Community Domain Coverage Gaps**

**1. BLIND SPOTS**  
The 12 entries collectively miss: (a) the role of **ritual, rites of passage, and calendrical rhythm** in sustaining belonging (no mention of how weekly Shabbat, monthly full-moon circles, or annual festivals create temporal glue beyond one-off reciprocity); (b) **developmental differences** — how community needs shift across life stages, especially adolescence, new parenthood, and bereavement; (c) **scale transitions** — how micro-communities (10–150 people) reliably interface with meso (1k–10k) and macro institutions without capture or dissolution; (d) **faith-based and cultural community infrastructure** that still serves ~60% of global population but is largely absent except in shadow warnings; (e) **online-native communities** that have achieved durable offline impact (e.g., effective altruism local chapters, rationalist houses, certain fandom-to-mutual-aid pipelines). The collection is heavily weighted toward Western secular, post-2010 experiments.

**2. MISSING BUILDERS**  
- **Robert Putnam & the Harwood Institute** — 25+ years translating "Bowling Alone" findings into civic revival toolkits used by 300+ U.S. communities.  
- **Priya Parker** (The Art of Gathering) — codified how to design transformative gatherings rather than default events; her methodology is now used by governments and corporations.  
- **Erika Poethig & the Urban Institute’s Community Resilience Framework** — rigorous longitudinal work on trauma-informed neighborhood governance.  
- **The Black Church** (specifically Interdenominational Theological Center and Faith in Action networks) — still the highest-trust, highest-participation infrastructure for millions of Americans; runs mutual aid, political power, and health navigation at scale.  
- **Naked House / Cobble Hill cohousing** and **Radical Faerie sanctuaries** — deliberate experiments in neurodivergent-friendly and queer-normative community design.  
- **Movement Strategy Center’s Translocal Organizing networks** — linking 200+ local justice communities into durable learning systems.

**3. SHADOW GAPS**  
Unaddressed shadows: (a) **status and reputation economies** inside gift systems — Buy Nothing groups often develop invisible hierarchies based on who gives the “best” items or most emotional labor; (b) **emotional labor displacement** onto women and non-binary people in “flat” reciprocity structures (documented in many intentional communities); (c) **exit costs** — leaving a tight community (especially network-state or CLT) can trigger total loss of social capital equivalent to leaving a cult; (d) **surveillance and accountability theater** in DAO/resilience-hub governance where transparency tools become tools of social control; (e) **aestheticization of poverty** — romantic use of “community” language to justify underfunded public services. The immune system is weakest on gendered emotional labor and status dynamics inside apparently horizontal structures.

**4. ACTIONABILITY GAPS** (most critical)  
A reader would still not know:  
- **Diagnostic**: What specific survey or 7-question audit reveals whether your current neighborhood has the preconditions for a viable reciprocity network?  
- **Sequencing**: Should you start with a Buy Nothing group, a third-place build, a land trust acquisition, or a resilience hub? Decision tree missing.  
- **Governance starter pack**: Exact charter language, default rules, conflict escalation ladder, and onboarding ritual that have >70% survival rate past 24 months (Ostrom is cited but no templates).  
- **Entry points by life stage** — what a 28-year-old vs. a 65-year-old should actually *do* on Monday morning.  
- **Metrics that matter**: Which 3–5 leading indicators (not lagging health outcomes) predict whether a community intervention will compound.  
- **Integration playbook**: How to connect a new CLT or DAO to existing municipal power, funding streams, and liability insurance without being crushed or co-opted.  
- **Personal practice**: Concrete micro-habits (beyond “give things away”) that reliably increase one’s own “community immune system” without burnout.  
The null result on actionability (+0.03) occurs because entries stay at high abstraction or single-case success porn; they lack **replicable unit operations**, failure autopsies, and branching decision frameworks.

**5. INTER-ENTRY TENSIONS**  
Sharpest tension is between **07–09 (network-state, cloud-first, cryptographic sovereignty)** and **10–12 (Ostrom, resilience hubs, CLTs)**. The former treats geography and legacy institutions as legacy OS to be routed around; the latter treats embedded physical communities and polycentric governance as non-negotiable. Gemini entries show high confidence in technological bypass (0.78–0.83); ChatGPT entries show measured confidence in institutional evolution (0.82–0.84). Timeline disagreement is implicit: network-state paradigm assumes rapid compilation (years); commons and CLT work assumes decades of patient institutional craft. No entry reconciles these ontologies.

**6. CONFIDENCE CALIBRATION**  
Scores are generally well-calibrated (0.78–0.95) on narrow empirical claims. Overconfidence appears in builder profiles and paradigm seeds (07, 09) that treat early-stage experiments (Zuzalu, Afropolitan, Gitcoin 2025 numbers) as durable signals rather than fragile pilots. Underconfidence nowhere obvious, but the entire collection is underconfident about the robustness of **ritual and developmental psychology** findings compared to the over-weighted quantitative epidemiology (Holt-Lunstad citations appear in 4 entries).
