Here is the assessment of the shelter domain’s coverage, focusing on actionability and reasoning gaps.

### 1. BLIND SPOTS
The collection is heavily indexed on structural construction tech (3D printing, modular, mycelium) and macro-financial critiques, but entirely misses **zoning, land-use reform, and the "Missing Middle."** There is zero mention of the YIMBY movement, the elimination of single-family zoning (e.g., Minneapolis 2040, California SB9), or the regulatory bottlenecks that make building illegal. Furthermore, it ignores **adaptive reuse** (converting vacant post-COVID commercial real estate into residential) and **climate-resilient shelter** (managed retreat, floating architecture, and rebuilding in uninsurable fire/flood zones). 

### 2. MISSING BUILDERS
*   **Dan Parolek (Opticos Design):** Coined the "Missing Middle" and provides actual architectural frameworks for building duplexes/courtyard apartments that bridge the gap between single-family homes and mid-rises.
*   **Brian Hanlon (California YIMBY) / Charles Marohn (Strong Towns):** The legislative and civic engineers actually rewriting the legal code to allow abundance.
*   **Michael Green (Michael Green Architecture):** Pioneering mass timber, a scalable, carbon-negative alternative to both concrete and mycelium.
*   **Atticus LeBlanc (PadSplit):** Utilizing existing housing stock by converting single-family homes into affordable co-living spaces, addressing immediate scarcity without new construction.

### 3. SHADOW GAPS
*   **The Insurance Crisis:** The collection ignores that climate risk is making vast swathes of housing uninsurable. A 3D-printed home is useless if the land it sits on cannot be insured against floods or wildfires, wiping out the owner's equity.
*   **Proprietary Vendor Lock-In:** If housing becomes a highly engineered "product" (modular/factory-built), we risk the "John Deere problem" for homes, where local tradesmen cannot legally or technically repair proprietary HVAC/electrical systems built into prefab walls.
*   **Resource Depletion:** 3D-printed concrete abundance ignores the looming global shortage of construction-grade sand.

### 4. ACTIONABILITY GAPS (The +0.03 Delta Explanation)
The benchmark showed no improvement in actionability because **these entries provide macro-observations without micro-levers.** An AI reading this knows that 3D printing and CLTs exist, but cannot tell a user *how to do anything about it*. 
To take action, a user needs:
*   **The ADU Playbook:** Step-by-step frameworks for navigating local permitting to build an Accessory Dwelling Unit in their backyard.
*   **Zoning Advocacy Mechanics:** How to audit a local municipal code and what specific amendments to demand at a city council meeting to legalize modular housing.
*   **Pro-Forma Financing:** How a small-scale developer actually secures a loan for a triplex when commercial banks only understand single-family or 200-unit apartment formulas. 
*   **CLT Initiation:** How a neighborhood actually incorporates a Community Land Trust, acquires its first parcel, and secures municipal grant funding. 
Without these, the entries are merely a museum of good ideas.
