Here is the assessment of the food domain’s collective blind spots, focusing on why the collection fails to generate actionable outputs.

### 1. BLIND SPOTS
The collection is heavily over-indexed on precision fermentation and ocean farming while completely missing critical immediate-term levers:
*   **Gene Editing in Traditional Crops:** CRISPR applications for climate resilience (e.g., flood-tolerant rice, drought-resistant wheat) are entirely absent. The entries jump from the 1960s Green Revolution straight to lab-grown proteins, skipping the biological upgrading of existing staple crops.
*   **Food Waste Upcycling & Logistics:** Entry 02 notes 1.05 billion tonnes of waste, but no entries explore the technologies solving this (e.g., automated sorting, shelf-life extension coatings, decentralized biodigesters).
*   **Regenerative Land Agriculture:** The entries critique the Green Revolution’s soil mining (Entry 03) and praise *ocean* regeneration (Entry 09), but completely omit the massive movement of regenerative *terrestrial* farming (cover cropping, rotational grazing). 

### 2. MISSING BUILDERS
*   **Pamela Ronald / UC Davis:** Pioneered Sub1 flood-tolerant rice, a massive leap in climate-resilient staple crops currently feeding millions.
*   **Apeel Sciences (James Rogers):** Developed plant-based protective coatings that double the shelf life of produce, directly attacking the 33% global food waste metric.
*   **Gabe Brown / Savory Institute:** The operational pioneers of terrestrial regenerative agriculture and holistic planned grazing, proving soil carbon sequestration at scale.
*   **Meati / Nature’s Fynd (Thomas Jonas):** Builders utilizing complex mycelium/fungi for whole-cut meat alternatives, bypassing the biological limitations of single-cell precision fermentation.
