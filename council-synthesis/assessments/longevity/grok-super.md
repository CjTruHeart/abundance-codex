**Assessment of Longevity Domain Coverage**

**1. BLIND SPOTS**  
The 12 entries collectively miss: (a) systems-level metabolic and mitochondrial interventions beyond epigenetic reprogramming (e.g., NAD+ salvage pathway failures, mitophagy enhancers like urolithin A, or senolytic pipelines); (b) the explosion of consumer longitudinal biomarkers (TruAge, DunedinPACE, GlycanAge, proteomics panels from Proteomics.com and Somalogic); (c) immune aging/immunosenescence as a master regulator (thymic involution, inflammaging, T-cell exhaustion); (d) reproductive longevity and ovarian aging (the 2024–2026 surge in AMH trajectory research and follicle activation trials); (e) canine-to-human translational learnings from Loyal, VCA, and the FDA’s acceptance of “reasonable expectation of effectiveness” for aging drugs; (f) polygenic risk scores + environmental interaction (e.g., UK Biobank + All of Us data showing 10–15 year modifiable gaps). The collection is heavily skewed toward epigenetic reprogramming and macro-trends, ignoring the multi-hallmark combinatorial reality.

**2. MISSING BUILDERS**  
- **Nir Barzilai** (Albert Einstein College of Medicine) – TAME trial (metformin, 2015–ongoing), targeting multiple hallmarks simultaneously.  
- **Unity Biotechnology** (Jamie Dananberg, Nathaniel David) – first senolytic clinical programs (UBX0101, UBX1325); Phase 2 eye disease data.  
- **Peter Attia** (Early Medical) – rigorous evidence-based clinical practice translating rapamycin, 17α-estradiol, acarbose, and continuous biomarker tracking into patient protocols.  
- **Laura Deming** (Longevity Fund, now The Longevity Fund II) – earliest institutional capital deployment into geroscience since 2015.  
- **Cynthia Kenyon** (Calico) – foundational daf-2/insulin/IGF-1 pathway work still driving small-molecule programs.  
- **Alex Zhavoronkov** (Insilico Medicine) – AI-discovered senolytic and fibrosis drugs reaching Phase 2.  
- **Reason** (Repair Biotechnologies) – thymic regeneration via FOXN1 and senolytic CAR-T programs.  
None of these appear.

**3. SHADOW GAPS**  
Unmentioned: (a) cancer risk from systemic reprogramming (the p53–pluripotency tradeoff is only hinted at); (b) delivery vector immunogenicity and repeat-dosing failure for AAV/OSK therapies; (c) massive incentive misalignment in the $400B+ supplement industry that has co-opted every longevity keyword; (d) fertility collapse risk if germline epigenetic resetting is required; (e) regulatory capture or outright ban scenarios if early reprogramming trials produce visible tumors; (f) the “longevity escape velocity” narrative’s fragility to one failed pivotal trial. The immune system’s weakest point is the complete absence of any entry addressing how to maintain adaptive immunity across an 120+ year lifespan.

**4. ACTIONABILITY GAPS** (most critical)

- How to build a personal 7–10 biomarker dashboard today (which labs, which tests: e.g., DunedinPACE + proteomics + coronary artery calcium score + VO2max + grip strength trajectory).

- Senolytic “hit-and-run” protocol evidence (dasatinib + quercetin intermittent schedules, current human data limitations).  
- How to evaluate a longevity clinic (red flags: full-body young plasma, unvalidated exosomes, $2M Bryan Johnson clones).  
- Prioritization matrix across the 12 hallmarks given limited time/money (e.g., exercise + sleep still dominate first 80 % of gains).

**5. INTER-ENTRY TENSIONS**  
Entry 01 and 06 treat the 12 hallmarks / Information Theory of Aging as near-settled science (high confidence), while Entry 12 correctly surfaces 2025–2026 papers showing clocks still lack rigorous clinical validation and may not outperform Framingham risk scores. **6. CONFIDENCE CALIBRATION**  
Scores are systematically overconfident on near-term human translation (0.88–0.95 on 2026 reprogramming trials, IND clearances, and “aging becomes programmable”). Historical translation failure rate from mouse longevity interventions to humans remains >95 %; the collection underweights this base rate. Underconfident on lifestyle effect sizes (Entry 03 cites 30–40 % mortality reduction from exercise yet frames it as “grift shadow” rather than primary intervention). Entry 10’s Blue Zones validation confidence (0.78) is appropriately cautious, but Entry 01’s 0.75 on the entire engineering paradigm feels too low given the cited evidence.
