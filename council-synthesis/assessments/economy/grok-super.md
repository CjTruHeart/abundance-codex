**Assessment of Economy Domain Coverage in the Abundance Codex**

**1. BLIND SPOTS**

The 12 entries collectively miss meso-level institutional innovation, particularly the redesign of corporations, capital allocation mechanisms, and ownership structures at scale. Absent are: 
- The explosion of alternative ownership (Employee Stock Ownership Plans (ESOPs), sovereign wealth funds for citizens, community land trusts, platform cooperatives beyond delivery).
- Modern monetary theory and fiscal innovation (e.g., how governments can issue currency for abundance infrastructure without triggering inflation when paired with supply-side deflation from AI/energy).
- The care economy and reproductive labor (unpaid work, demographic collapse in high-income countries, declining fertility as economic signal).
- Biodiversity and natural capital accounting at the macroeconomic level.
- Defense and geopolitical economy (how conflict economies and military Keynesianism interact with abundance transitions).
- The data economy and attention/experience markets as primary arenas of value creation.

**2. MISSING BUILDERS**

- **Marjorie Kelly** (Democracy Collaborative): Pioneered "Next System" work and "The Making of a Democratic Economy," advancing employee ownership, anchor institution strategies, and community wealth building; her frameworks have influenced over 100 U.S. cities.
- **Rohan Silva** and **Public.io**: Building open-source civic technology and new institutions for public goods creation outside traditional government/corporate paradigms.
- **Carlota Perez** (theorist + practitioner influence): Her techno-economic paradigm shifts framework directly informs how finance must be redirected during deployment periods; missing entirely.
- **Mazzucato's** Institute for Innovation and Public Purpose and her "missions" approach to directed abundance (though one mention of her ideas is indirect).
- **Dan Ariely's** work on irrationality-informed institutional design and **Danielle Allen's** "difference without domination" applied to economic governance.
- **Open Source Pharma Foundation** and **Innocentive**-style prize funds for directed innovation.
- **Singapore's** Temasek and Norway's Government Pension Fund Global as sovereign vehicles repurposed for abundance infrastructure.

**3. SHADOW GAPS**

The collection under-addresses:
- **Coordination failures at planetary scale** (global public goods problem, free-rider dynamics on abundance infrastructure like open-source AI, energy, and biotech).
- **Status anxiety and meaning collapse** as primary political economy drivers (beyond labor identity); how zero-sum status games persist even in material abundance.
- **Regulatory capture by new abundance incumbents** (AI labs, energy hyperscalers, data monopolists becoming harder to dislodge than legacy banks).
- **Fertility collapse and human capital deflation** — the ultimate economic shadow if abundance reduces the perceived economic value of having children.
- **Epistemic capture**: When narrative control becomes the highest-leverage economic activity, truth-seeking institutions erode.
- **Weaponization of abundance** (AI-enabled autonomous weapons economies, biological weapons, or memetic warfare making coordination impossible).

The immune system is weakest against "abundance for me, scarcity for thee" dynamics and elite overproduction leading to intra-elite conflict.

**4. ACTIONABILITY GAPS**

A reader would still not know:
- **Decision framework**: How to distinguish between genuine rails-building, rent-extraction rebranded as "DPI," and extractive platforms. No checklist for evaluating whether a new "open rail" is truly neutral and forkable.
- **Capital allocation heuristics**: Concrete patterns for directing marginal capital toward deflationary technologies versus inflationary assets. No updated "barbell strategy" for the abundance transition.
- **Institutional entrepreneurship playbook**: Specific moves for creating new economic institutions (e.g., how to charter a new kind of corporation with baked-in abundance mandates, how to design a citizen sovereign wealth fund, how to structure a "public option" that doesn't decay).
- **Personal/corporate first moves**: What a mid-career professional, city mayor, or foundation program officer should actually do on Monday. No "minimum viable abundance project" templates.
- **Measurement transition**: Exact proxies and dashboards for moving from GDP to "cost of flourishing" metrics at organizational or municipal scale.
- **Negotiation with legacy systems**: How to handle pension funds, insurance companies, and legacy balance sheets tied to scarcity assets without causing systemic collapse.

This explains the null result on actionability (+0.03): the entries celebrate outcomes and paradigms but provide almost zero "how-to" reasoning patterns or decision rubrics. They inspire but do not transmit craft.

**5. INTER-ENTRY TENSIONS**

Strong tension between **07-commons-asset-paradigm-seed** (survival as network-maintained baseline, near-zero costs via public/shared infrastructure) and **09-post-labor-valuation-matrix-contrast** (which still assumes some market/valuation layer) versus the more pragmatic rail-building entries (04-06) that accept corporate and state roles. 

**11-infinity-economy** (confidence 0.58) undercuts the empirical grounding of the Great Enrichment (02, 0.92) by leaping to "intelligence scales faster than scarcity" without addressing the persistent physical dependencies named in 08's shadow check. The collection is simultaneously too optimistic about technological deflation and too pessimistic about institutional invention.

**6. CONFIDENCE CALIBRATION**

Scores are mostly well-calibrated for evidence anchors (0.8-0.95) but systematically overconfident in narrative framing. The 0.58 on "infinity economy" is appropriately low, but entries like 06 ("the rail is the revolution") at 0.78 overstate the sufficiency of open rails without addressing governance decay rates. The collection under-calibrates on transition pain duration and political feasibility, treating institutional questions as implementation details rather than the primary constraint.

While reskilling, universal basic income pilots, and progressive taxation are discussed, evidence for their effectiveness at scale is mixed. Ultimately, it's a complex, wicked problem requiring coordinated action across national and supranational levels that rarely materializes. Cities should focus on social cohesion and managing expectations as much as economic strategy." (98 words)
