## What Spec A Proved (By Failing)

### Five Interventions, Zero Movement on R1 Accuracy

| Version | Intervention | R1 Accuracy Delta | Moved? |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|--------|
| v2.1 | (baseline scaffolds, no R1-targeted intervention) | −0.214 | — |
| v2.2 | Confidence floor (0.75) | −0.226 | No |
| v2.2 | Metadata stripping (conf=) | −0.226 | No |
| v2.2 | System prompt hardening | −0.226 | No |
| v2.3 | Retriever year-relabeling (≥2025 → "recent data") | **−0.262** | No (worse) |
| v2.3 | System prompt softening ("source and time period") | **−0.262** | No |
| v2.3 | Judge preamble (attempted) | — | **Dropped** after 2 calibration rounds failed orthogonal controls |

Five interventions across two release cycles. All directionally aimed at the same diagnostic hypothesis (post-cutoff data being read as fabrication by the judge). None moved the metric. The v2.3 number is the lowest of the five.

### Root Cause Confirmed: Rubric Limitation

The `accuracy` criterion as written conflates two distinct properties:

- **Verifiable**: Can the judge confirm this claim against its training data?
- **Plausible**: Is this claim consistent with known trends and cited sources?

RAG-augmented responses systematically surface recent, specific, well-sourced data that judges cannot verify against their own training cutoff. The more the dataset does its job — retrieving current, specific, institutionally-sourced evidence — the more the accuracy criterion penalizes it.

Judge preamble calibration (two rounds, committed at `2451c99` and `afdc6e1`) demonstrated this is not tunable within the current rubric:

- **Round 1** (permissive, "institutional publishers" halo): passed 11/11 regression flips, but 2/5 FALSE POSITIVES on invented-publication controls
- **Round 2** (restrictive, "confirm publisher from training"): 0/5 invented-FP PASS, but only 1/3 regression flips — over-rejected legitimate post-cutoff citations

The two failure modes are on opposite sides of the same axis. Per the pre-registered rule ("two rounds is rigorous; three is overfitting to the control set"), the preamble was dropped rather than tuned a third time.

This is a **structural limitation of LLM-as-judge evaluation for any RAG system with post-cutoff data**, not specific to the Abundance Codex. Five interventions failing in the same direction is not a data problem; it is a measurement problem.

**v2.4 mandate:** Redesign the accuracy criterion to separate verifiable from plausible. See `TIER-2B-SPECS.md` Spec 2B-A.

## What v2.3 Means for the Technical Report

The R3 confirmation closes the paper's central arc — the dataset's effect on Ring 3 (derived reasoning) advanced from null (v2.0) through inconclusive (v2.1) through missed-by-fractions (v2.2) to pre-registered-confirmed (v2.3), with a falsifiable intervention (pillar-gated empowerment) moving the metric into the predicted band.

The R1 falsification opens a distinct methodological contribution about LLM-as-judge limitations for RAG evaluation: when augmented responses surface verifiable-but-post-cutoff data, binary accuracy rubrics conflate "unverifiable from judge's training" with "incorrect." Five targeted interventions across two release cycles failed to separate these. The rubric redesign (Spec 2B-A) will test whether splitting the criterion recovers the measurement.

Both findings are publishable. The dataset works; the measurement instrument has a documented, characterized limitation that applies to the entire field, not just this project.
