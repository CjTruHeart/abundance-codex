**Domain:** Computation Intelligence | **Type:** Star Trek Spec | **Status:** Curated

## One-Line Essence
> Ubiquitous creation conversation — any human describes what they need, and it exists. The scarce resource is clarity of intention; capability is abundant, ambient, and responsive.

---

## The Civilization-Scale Vision

### What It Looks Like

You're a teacher in 2045. You describe a simulation for explaining photosynthesis to 8-year-olds. In minutes, it exists — interactive, accurate, beautiful. You test it with your class. You describe refinements. It evolves. You didn't "code." You conversed. The simulation is yours, but you never touched syntax.

You're a farmer. You describe a tool for predicting irrigation needs based on soil sensors and weather. It exists. You use it. You describe improvements after the first season. It evolves. You own the tool; you own the iteration.

You're a child. You describe a game you imagined. It exists. You play it. You describe changes. It evolves. You are a creator because you have intention, not because you apprenticed for years.

### What It Feels Like

**Effortless:** Not "easy" — clarity is still hard. But the translation from intention to reality is frictionless.

**Empowering:** Not dependent on gatekeepers, employers, or technical priests. Direct connection between need and solution.

**Conversational:** Natural language, iterative refinement, partnership rather than command.

**Ubiquitous:** Expected, ambient, invisible. Like writing — once specialized, now universal.

### What It Enables

- **Localized Solutions:** Every community builds tools for its specific context
- **Rapid Response:** Solutions emerge in hours, not quarters
- **Creative Expression:** Software as medium, like writing or video
- **Democratic Participation:** Technical capability no longer concentrates power
